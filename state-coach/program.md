# Training program — Tim — cut phase (2026-06-22 → ~2026-12-31)

## Philosophy

- **Aggressive fat loss, muscle retention.** The fat comes off via the deficit; muscle stays
  by keeping the weights heavy and protein high. No objective breaks: strength holds,
  conditioning builds, weight trends down ~0.65 kg/week.
- Operating thresholds live in `state.json` and SOUL.md, applied automatically.
- Three lift days, cardio before each. Shoulders and arms are folded into the Pull/Push days
  so nothing gets neglected without adding training days.

## Weekly structure (microcycle)

| Day | Cardio (30–45 min, before lift) | Lift |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Z2 (incline walk / bike / row) | **Pull** — Deadlift, Lat pulldown, Assisted pull-up, Seated cable row, Back extension, Biceps curl |
| Tue | rest (optional walk, hit 8k steps) | — |
| **Wed** | Z2 (easy — don't pre-fatigue legs) | **Legs** — Squat, Leg press, Lunges, Leg extension, Lying leg curl, (Calf raise) |
| Thu | rest (optional walk) | — |
| **Fri** | Z2, or short intervals for BJJ wind | **Push** — Bench, Incline/Decline press, Overhead press, Lateral raise, Triceps pushdown |
| Sat | rest / active recovery | — |
| Sun | rest | — |

### Why this layout
- **Shoulders + arms solved:** biceps live on Pull day (they assist every pull), shoulders +
  triceps on Push day (they assist every press). Classic, efficient, no extra days.
- **Pre-lift cardio stays Z2** so it doesn't steal performance from the lift — the lift is
  what protects muscle in a deficit. Keep it conversational-pace: incline treadmill, bike,
  or rower. Save genuine high-intensity intervals for when BJJ conditioning becomes the
  focus, or do them after lifting, not before leg day.

## Strength progression (in a cut)

- Main lifts (deadlift, squat, bench): work in a **4–6 rep strength range**. Progress weight
  when all working sets are clean. Holding or slowly progressing strength *while losing fat*
  is a win — don't chase PRs.
- Accessories: **double progression** — stay in the rep range (8–12, 12–15 for isolation),
  add weight when you hit the top of the range on all sets.
- If a main lift fails its target reps **2 sessions running**: check protein + sleep first.
  If both are fine, deload that lift −10% and rebuild. (This is the muscle-loss alarm.)

## Cardio / conditioning toward BJJ

- Base: 30–45 min Z2 before each lift = ~90–135 min/week of easy aerobic work. Builds the
  aerobic base BJJ needs and adds to the deficit.
- As weight drops and the base builds, layer in 1 weekly conditioning piece (intervals,
  rounds-style) — ideally after a lift or on a rest day, not before legs.
- Apple Watch: report duration + avg HR (and calories if you want); Arnold logs it.

## Nutrition

- **≈1950 kcal/day base · P185 / F55 / C175.** Protein non-negotiable — it's what keeps the
  muscle. Hit it every day.
- Carb cycling: **+35 g carb on training days** (fuel the lift), base on rest days.
- Hydration: 3 L base + ~500 ml per cardio session.
- Steps: 8,000/day, every day.
- **Alcohol: zero.** Every drink is logged honestly; the dry streak is tracked.
- Daily fasted weigh-in → 7-day moving average drives all calorie adjustments.

## Phase exit

At ~70 kg (or when the 7d MA reaches ~73 kg), slow the deficit, raise calories toward
maintenance, and shift the emphasis from "lose fat" to "BJJ conditioning + recomposition."
Revisit macros and rep schemes then.
